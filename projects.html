Abyssian

Overview

Abyssian is a first-person, action-adventure metroid-vania developed in Unity engine as a Capstone project in a small team of 11 developers/designers. With an equal emphasis on exploration, puzzle-solving, and alien combat, Abyssian takes place on an alient planet overcome by an evil presence. Play as a lone survivor in this hostile crashsite, looking for any way out.

Main Contributions

As one of the four programmers in the team, I mainly focused on developing core game mechanics, including weapon functionality, player UI and stats, AI behaviour, animations, and more.

    Player UI / Player Stats

  • implemented player HUD, that includes ammo, health and armor display, current weapons equipped, notifications, and compass component
  • ammo system UI updates corresponding to player's current ammo count and current weapons clip size
  • compass component that tracks nearby enemies, ammo and health pickups, icons grow/shrink based on proximity
  • on screen notifications for ammo and health pickups
  • health, armor and stamina UI display utilizes Unity sliders and reflects player's current stats
  • reticule that targets enemies and grows/shrinks on focus, also displays current item on raycast

    Enemy Animations

  • programmed the animation states and transitions using artists' fbx files for the enemy and boss models

    • AI Behaviour

  • programmed AI logic for roamer enemies and boss using finite state machines
  • roamer enemy wanders between control points until player is in range/sight distance, then enters a chase state until it is close enough to attack
  • boss enemy switches between randomized attacks; homing, drones, and close-range, and enters a "stunned" state upon taking significant damage, giving player a chance to hit their weak spot

    Weapon Mechanics

  • programmed the base class for weapon functionality, using raycasting and particle effects to simulate projectiles instead of object pooling
  • each weapon has different functionality and it's own ammo count, types and clip sizes
  • implemented the artists' animations for weapon use and switching
